If a child can spot a shoplifter, a machine can too

A let's try moment turned into 8 months of specialization and breaking a record for caffine intake

That was the starting analogy into creating a Machine Learning model. The goal was to detect shoplifting behavior in real-time, just like breaking a complex skill down into simple and detectable patterns as if I was teaching a child to spot theft incidents.

The process involved training a Long-Short-Term Memory model. Think about it as a Recurrent-Neural-Network with a working memory and a variety of operations to preserve patterns over time.

It required breaking down the theft behavior into short, labeled sequences and leveraging powerful computer vision tools. Deep integration of cv2 (OpenCV) for frame processing and MediaPipe (Google) for precise body-parts tracking allowed me to generate neat feature vectors that accurately quantify human behavior.

What sounds like a simple concept required a massive undertaking of code, library integration, deep diving into research papers, and tuning the hyperparameters to get the first successful test. This entire project was a 8-month deep dive in one of the most exciting fields of Computer Science.

In order to make it useful, just connect the model to a face recognition model and a real-time listener triggering a callback to store the alert in the cloud.

I used Firebase for quick SDKs and configurations over a React Native mobile app and here we go with two more features, first is shoplifter identification and second is a live incident report summary on your phone screen.

I will be posting detailed follow-ups soon that simplify the specifics of the model architecture and data preparation. Let me know what you are curious about in this journey by commenting below
